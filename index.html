About me:

I'm a Computer Science undergraduate student at the Institute of Mathematics and Statistics (IME-USP), where students have the freedom to choose specific paths within the field (https://bcc.ime.usp.br/trilhas/), and even combine them if they wish.
After taking the course MAC0102 — designed to help students explore and choose one or more of these specialization tracks — I decided to fully pursue the Software and Artificial Intelligence tracks. The Software track appealed to me because it provides a comprehensive understanding of how programming evolves into real-world applications. It covers not only the development process but also the broader context of software as a product: design, deployment, user experience, and project lifecycle.
The AI track, on the other hand, reflects the current and future landscape of technology. With artificial intelligence becoming increasingly integrated into everyday tools and services, having a solid grasp of AI concepts is essential. Moreover, combining AI with Software opens the door to developing intelligent and user-friendly applications — a synergy that strongly motivates me.
Finally, it's worth noting that my choice of these tracks doesn't prevent me from exploring other fields, such as Data Science and Theoretical Computer Science, which I also find fascinating.
